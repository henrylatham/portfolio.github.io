BackTracker

Product Manager & founder at a travel start-up

-

Problem

Whilst backpacking in rural Brazil for 3 months, it quickly became apparent that there was nothing online that specifically catered for the 30m annual backpacking market.

Physical travel guides were inevitably out-dated and detached, lacking the social validation & user-generated content we are now accustomed to consuming.

Facebook allowed you to stay in touch, yes, but failed to cater for the dynamic nature of backpacking.

Where are other backpackers? Can I see the routes & suggestions of these backpackers? Can I meet up with new backpackers or ones I’ve met before?

Apart from the concept, the major problem was a lack of technical knowledge. Where to start?

Thus, during my final year at university, I spent the entire year learning to design & manage web & app builds. I eventually brought in a team of five developers, a business development manager & content manager.

Ideation & Role

Having played around with ideas numerous times during my travels, I struck upon a final concept once back in the UK:

Plot your journey, track your friends & share your experiences.

The idea was to marry a dynamic, map-based blog with a trail of user-generated suggestions in all backpacking destinations.

We decided to start with an iOS app, as the majority of our market had an iPhone5 or 6.

As many backpackers still used hostel computers, we also built a simple web app in order to allow the whole market to access BackTracker.

This was key, as the app would only gain traction if a) significant hubs were created in popular destinations and b) the majority was able to access the platform.

My role evolved throughout our 2-year involvement with BackTracker.

I started as CEO, managing content, a development team and our growth strategy - as well as working as our principle designer.

After a year, however, I realised that this was too much and, if I wanted to master one skill and do it well, it must be design.

I therefore stepped down in order to focus on product development, honing my UX/UI skills, technical knowledge and managing the development team more closely.

As product ownerand manager, I had the vision for the app’s value and development timeline, managing an Android, iOS, web & backend development team to make this happen.

I also conducted qualitative research into user behaviour, user needs & regular usability testing in hostels or over Skype.

I was also assisted by the mentorship of a handful of designers, product managers & entrepreneurs.

Execution

Within 10 days of launching we were featured under the ‘Best New Apps’ category in a number of European countries, including the UK.

Finishing final year exams a few weeks later then allowed us to focus on iteration. With the 12,000 downloads we had, we were able to delve into the data and iterate the product.

We decided to create a leaner product, cutting the journey plotting feature temporarily in order to focus on what we felt was the main hook for users: user-generated, backpacker-specific content.

Using the Hook Canvas model, I focused my efforts on first hooking users in with relevant content, which we determined to be unique suggestions near them - and available - right now.

The focus then shifted towards encouraging user investment (adding their own suggestions, interacting with existing ones, etc.)

In early 2016, the product was acquired by a VC-backed company in the UK, who are currently rebuilding the product with plans to re-launch later this year.
